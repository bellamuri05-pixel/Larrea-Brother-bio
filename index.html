Larrea Brothers – Vicente and Antonio Larrea – are Chilean designers whose work blends political commitment, cultural pride, and bold visual experimentation. Growing up in a country undergoing major social and political change, they became key voices in shaping the visual identity of Chile’s Popular Unity movement during Salvador Allende’s presidency. Influenced by modernism, pop-art color, Chilean folk imagery, and the urgency of speaking to large public audiences, their posters used vibrant palettes, geometric symbols, and clear messaging to communicate optimism, solidarity, and resistance. Working through different mediums – print, collage, photography, and early digital typesetting – they created visuals that helped unify communities and preserve collective memory. Especially in the face of censorship during the Pinochet dictatorship. Their dedication to design as a tool for representation and political expression encourages others to take creative risks, honor cultural context, and recognize the responsibility designers have in uplifting marginalized voices.
